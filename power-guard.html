High-frequency energy analytics and forecasting.
EU CBAM & DPP Compliant.

THE ENERGY CRISIS

Factories in Gazipur often operate at <30% capacity due to chronic LNG shortages. To maintain uptime, they rely on captive diesel generation which costs $0.32/kWh—nearly triple the grid rate of $0.12/kWh. Unmonitored machinery contributes to a 12-15% energy overspend.

Peak Shaving Strategy: Using the PZEM-004T split-core sensor ($9), we monitor power quality at 1Hz. The system predicts load spikes 180 seconds in advance, allowing for automated "load shedding" of non-critical systems (like ACs) to prevent tripping generators. This saves roughly $180/month per line.

ANALYTICS PIPELINE

  • VECTORIZATION

    TS2Vec + MOMEMTO. Raw power data is noisy. We use TS2Vec to convert time-series data into vector embeddings. The MOMEMTO (Memory Gate) patch is crucial—it prevents the model from "forgetting" baseline behavior over weeks of operation (Concept Drift), ensuring stable anomaly detection without constant retraining.

  • CHANGE POINT

    Bayesian CPD. We use Bayesian Change Point Detection algorithms to identify structural shifts in energy consumption (e.g., a motor seizing up or a belt slipping) distinct from normal fluctuations.

  • COMPLIANCE

    Digital Product Passport. Energy logs are saved in immutable Parquet format, providing the granular data required for EU CBAM audits.
