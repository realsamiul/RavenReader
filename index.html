01. THE MACRO CONTEXT

LABOR ARBITRAGE IS OVER.

The era of relying solely on low wages is ending. With US duties stabilizing at 20% (down from a threatened 37%), the comparative advantage of Bangladeshi labor has eroded against near-shoring competitors like Mexico, which benefit from faster logistics.

Simultaneously, the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) phases in by January 2026. This requires auditable energy traces for every garment. Factories that cannot digitally prove their efficiency will face tariff penalties.

The "Retrofit Innovation" Strategy: Traditional "Industry 4.0" requires replacing machinery with smart units ($12,000+ CAPEX). We utilize a "Brownfield" approach—deploying commodity edge compute nodes (like Raspberry Pis) to overlay intelligence onto existing, "dumb" infrastructure without stopping the line.

02. THE PLATFORM

EDGE-NATIVE INTELLIGENCE

Three drop-in AI modules. Zero cloud dependency. $150 Hardware BOM. 47-day ROI.

COMPUTE SOVEREIGNTY

Our architecture rejects the centralized cloud model. We utilize the Raspberry Pi 5 (quad-core 2.4GHz) and recycled Android Smartphones (Snapdragon 778G) as local compute nodes.

Why Edge? Streaming HD video to the cloud incurs latency (>3000ms) that is too slow for high-speed fabric inspection. By running 8-bit quantized models locally, we achieve <200ms latency. This also guarantees Data Sovereignty—proprietary patterns and worker faces are processed in RAM and never transmit to third-party servers, ensuring inherent GDPR/DSA compliance.
