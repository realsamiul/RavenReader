01. THE MACRO CONTEXT

THE END OF LABOR ARBITRAGE.

The comparative advantage of Bangladeshi labor is eroding. With US duties stabilizing at 20% (down from a threatened 37%), Bangladesh faces stiff competition from "Near-Shoring" hubs like Mexico and Turkey, which offer speed-to-market that offsets their higher wages.

Simultaneously, the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) phases in by January 2026. This is a non-tariff barrier that requires auditable energy traces for every garment. Factories that cannot digitally prove their efficiency will face tariff penalties or exclusion.

"A 1% improvement in quality or uptime is no longer a marginal gain; in a $32.6B export market, it is a $326M defense mechanism."

03. ARCHITECTURE

EDGE-NATIVE INTELLIGENCE

COMPUTE SOVEREIGNTY

Our architecture utilizes the Raspberry Pi 5 (quad-core 2.4GHz) and recycled Android Smartphones (Snapdragon 778G) as local compute nodes.

Why Edge? Streaming HD video to the cloud incurs latency (>3000ms) that is too slow for high-speed fabric inspection. By running 8-bit quantized models locally, we achieve <200ms latency.
