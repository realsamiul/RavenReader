QC VISION

Real-time fabric defect detection on commodity edge hardware.
28ms Inference. 92% F1 Score.

FRUGAL INNOVATION

The Economic Leakage: RMG factories currently lose 3-5% of total yardage to defects (oil stains, holes, broken stitches). Traditional solutions—imported Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) rigs from brands like KeyeTech—cost upwards of $12,000 per unit, making them inaccessible for most SMEs. [attachment_0](attachment)

The Disruption: We utilize a "Retrofit" strategy. By leveraging recycled Android smartphones (with Snapdragon 778G+ processors) or Raspberry Pi 5 units as vision sensors, we lower the hardware Bill of Materials (BOM) to roughly $150. This democratizes high-precision QC, allowing factories to digitize inspection without heavy capital expenditure.

  • BACKBONE: MICROVIT

    MicroViT-Tiny-Q8. We moved away from the heavier DINO-v2 model. By using MicroViT and applying 8-bit integer quantization (compressing 32-bit floating point math), we run Transformers directly on the CPU. This achieves a 3.6x speedup while retaining a 91.3% accuracy on fabric datasets.

  • SEGMENTATION: PICOSAM-2

    Human-in-the-Loop. Instead of the heavy Segment Anything Model (SAM), we use PicoSAM-2 (1.3M parameters). This allows "Promptable Masks"—a QA manager can simply tap a new defect type on a tablet screen, and the model instantly learns the defect boundary, enabling rapid retraining on the factory floor.
